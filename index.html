Data Classification: Voice volume (dB) and ambient sound (dB) are acoustic measurements transmitted as raw values — equivalent to a sound level meter. 🔊 dBFS → dBA Converter
The watch microphone reads in dBFS (decibels relative to digital full scale — 0 dBFS = maximum digital signal, all values are negative). To convert to dBA (A-weighted decibels — the standard acoustic scale), add the microphone sensitivity constant and the SLM calibration offset.
Approximate dBA
80.5 dBA
dBA ≈ dBFS + mic sensitivity + calibration offset. This is an approximation — actual dBA depends on frequency content and A-weighting curve. Sufficient for classroom voice volume observation.
📐 SLM Calibration Offset Calculator
How to calibrate: Play a steady sound (pink noise from a speaker). Place the watch and a reference Sound Level Meter at the same distance from the source. Read both at the same moment. Enter the values below. The calculator tells you what offset to enter on the watch's Quick Offset screen. The watch accepts offsets in 0.5 dB increments — the calculator rounds to the nearest 0.5.
Calibration Offset to Enter on Watch
+2.5 dBA
The reference SLM reads 2.5 dBA higher than the watch. Enter +2.5 on the watch's Quick Offset screen (tap + five times from 0.0). The watch will add 2.5 dBA to every reading going forward.
Recommended reference SLMs: NIOSH SLM app (free, validated by the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health — the only phone-based SLM with published accuracy data), or any Class 2 or Class 1 dedicated sound level meter. Calibrate in a quiet room with a steady pink noise source at moderate volume (70-80 dBA).
